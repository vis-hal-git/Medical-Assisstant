Emergency Information

Know when to seek immediate medical attention

Emergency Services

Call 911 (US) or your local emergency number

India: 112 | UK: 999 | EU: 112 | Australia: 000

Call Emergency Services Immediately If:

Heart Attack Signs

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Pain radiating to arm, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweats, nausea

Stroke Signs (FAST)

  • Face drooping
  • Arm weakness
  • Speech difficulty
  • Time to call emergency

Breathing Emergency

  • Severe difficulty breathing
  • Choking
  • Blue lips or face
  • Drowning

Severe Bleeding

  • Uncontrollable bleeding
  • Deep wounds
  • Coughing or vomiting blood
  • Blood in stool (large amounts)

Severe Allergic Reaction

  • Swelling of face/throat
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Dizziness or fainting

Trauma & Injuries

  • Severe burns
  • Head injuries with confusion
  • Suspected broken bones
  • Electric shock

Poisoning

  • Ingested toxic substances
  • Drug overdose
  • Carbon monoxide exposure
  • Call Poison Control

Neurological

  • Seizures
  • Sudden severe headache
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Sudden confusion

Basic First Aid Tips

CPR Basics
  1. Call emergency services first
  2. Place heel of hand on center of chest
  3. Push hard and fast (100-120 compressions/min)
  4. Allow chest to fully recoil between compressions
  5. If trained, give 2 rescue breaths every 30 compressions
Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)
  1. Stand behind the person
  2. Place fist above navel, thumb side in
  3. Grasp fist with other hand
  4. Give quick upward thrusts
  5. Repeat until object is expelled
Bleeding Control
  1. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
  2. Keep the wound elevated if possible
  3. Don't remove cloth if soaked - add more
  4. Apply tourniquet only for life-threatening limb bleeding
  5. Seek medical attention
Burns
  1. Cool the burn under cool running water for 10-20 minutes
  2. Remove jewelry/tight items near burn
  3. Cover loosely with sterile bandage
  4. Don't apply ice, butter, or ointments
  5. Seek medical care for severe burns
